Why this is not another ChatGPT book

Origin

Autonomous Agent was created with Fable 5 and Cursor. The work unfolded as a series of long agent sessions: missions, structure, verification, rewriting, and honest postmortems.

This is not a collection of prompt tricks or a marketing brochure. It is a working draft about moving from one-off requests to a system where AI can carry meaningful work over time.

For whom

You already use ChatGPT, Claude, or Cursor every day, and you can feel the ceiling. AI speeds up answers, but you remain the dispatcher of every step.

The book teaches a different mode: set missions, not prompts. Build an engine of autonomous work: memory, cycle, verification, repair, and protection from the false appearance of progress.

"On Wednesday at 7:40, I opened my laptop and read a report about work I had not done. Overnight, the agent closed four questions in the research. I had not written a prompt the evening before. I had not talked to it for two days."
- from the introduction

The Mission Engine - 6 Strokes

Not a bag of tips, but one system. A running case follows a month-long research mission from a client email to the final report, including the failures.

  1. Mission - outcome, boundaries, and the definition of done
  2. Task memory - state outside the context window
  3. Step - planning and acting inside one cycle
  4. Verification - did it get better, or just longer?
  5. Continuation - the next valuable step without your nudge
  6. Repair - fix contradictions instead of amplifying the prompt

Five Laws of the Core

  • A mission without a definition of done never ends.
  • The context window is not memory. It is a draft in the wind.
  • The appearance of progress is more dangerous than stopping.
  • A mission is held by external state, not by the model's memory.
  • A one-off mission is heroics. A mission that leaves tools behind is a system.
